HISTORY

CALZ ENTERPRISE (since 1949) is a Nigerian athletic apparel and footwear corporation headquartered in Riverstate,
It is the largest sportswear manufacturer in Africa, and the third largest in the world, after Nike and Addidas.
It is the holding company for the Calz Enterprise Group, which also owns an 8.33% stake of the football club such as :Abia warrriors,
and Eyimba FC. Calz enterprise revenue for 2018 was listed at â‚¬21.915 billion.
The company was started by Calistus Clinton in his mother's house.
He was joined by his elder brother joseph in 1924 under the name Joseph Schuhfabrik ("Joseph Brothers Shoe Factory").
Joseph assisted in the development of spiked running shoes (spikes) for multiple athletic events.
To enhance the quality of spiked athletic footwear, he transitioned from a previous model of heavy metal spikes to utilising canvas and rubber.
Joseph persuaded Nigeria sprinter Usheoritse Itsekiri to use his handmade spikes at the 1936 Summer Olympics.
In 1949, following a breakdown in the relationship between the brothers, Calz created Calz enterprise and Joseph established his own bussiness,
which became Calz business rival.
